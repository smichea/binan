## Pre-Colonial Era / Panahong Pre-Kolonyal

### Vocabulary and Grammar Explanation

---

**1.**  
**Original:**  
> *Bago pa man dumating ang mga Espanyol, ang lupain ng Bi√±an ay bahagi ng sinaunang pamayanan ng Tabuco (na ngayon ay Cabuyao).*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **bago pa man** ‚Äì *even before*  
- **dumating** ‚Äì *arrived*  
- **mga Espanyol** ‚Äì *the Spaniards*  
- **lupain** ‚Äì *land*  
- **bahagi** ‚Äì *part*  
- **sinaunang pamayanan** ‚Äì *ancient settlement* (root: *sinauna* = ancient, *pamayanan* = community)  
- **Tabuco** ‚Äì *old name* (now Cabuyao)  
- **na ngayon ay** ‚Äì *which is now*

**Grammar:**  
- Passive past construction.  
- Apposition: *Tabuco (na ngayon ay Cabuyao)* explains and updates Tabuco's identity.

**Translation:**  
> Even before the Spaniards arrived, the land of Bi√±an was part of the ancient settlement of Tabuco (which is now Cabuyao).

---

**2.**  
**Original:**  
> *Walang maituturing na bayan noon sa Bi√±an ‚Äì kakaunti at magkakalayo ang mga naninirahan, at payak ang kanilang pamumuhay.*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **walang** ‚Äì *none, no*  
- **maituturing** ‚Äì *could be considered* (root: *turing* = regard, consider)  
- **bayan** ‚Äì *town*  
- **noon** ‚Äì *at that time*  
- **kakaunti** ‚Äì *few, sparse*  
- **magkakalayo** ‚Äì *scattered, distant from each other* (root: *layo* = distance)  
- **nananinirahan** ‚Äì *residents* (root: *tirahan* = place to live)  
- **payak** ‚Äì *simple, plain*  
- **kanilang pamumuhay** ‚Äì *their way of life* (root: *buhay* = life)

**Grammar:**  
- *Walang maituturing na bayan* ‚Üí no town could really be considered existing.  
- Conjunction *‚Äì* (dash) used to link related ideas.  
- Use of repetitive-sounding words (kakaunti, magkakalayo) is very natural in Tagalog for emphasis.

**Translation:**  
> There could not yet be considered a town in Bi√±an ‚Äî there were few and scattered residents, and their way of life was simple.

---

**3.**  
**Original:**  
> *Umaasa sila sa biyaya ng kalikasan: nangingisda sila sa Lawa ng Laguna, nangunguha ng prutas at halamang-ugat sa gubat, at nangangaso ng baboy-ramo at iba pang hayop sa kaparangan.*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **umaasa** ‚Äì *they rely* (root: *asa* = to hope or depend)  
- **sila** ‚Äì *they*  
- **biyaya** ‚Äì *blessing, bounty*  
- **kalikasan** ‚Äì *nature*  
- **nangingisda** ‚Äì *fishing*  
- **nangunguha** ‚Äì *gathering* (root: *kuha* = to get)  
- **prutas** ‚Äì *fruits*  
- **halamang-ugat** ‚Äì *root crops* (plants like yam, cassava)  
- **gubat** ‚Äì *forest*  
- **nangangaso** ‚Äì *hunting* (root: *gaso* = hunt)  
- **baboy-ramo** ‚Äì *wild boar*  
- **iba pang hayop** ‚Äì *other animals*  
- **kaparangan** ‚Äì *grassland, open fields*

**Grammar:**  
- Colon *:* introduces a list of activities.  
- Parallel structure: **nangingisda, nangunguha, nangangaso** all present tense, simultaneous actions.

**Translation:**  
> They relied on nature's bounty: they fished in Laguna Lake, gathered fruits and root crops in the forest, and hunted wild boar and other animals in the grasslands.

---

**4.**  
**Original:**  
> *Ang ugnayan ng mga tao ay umiikot sa mga barangay na pinamumunuan ng mga lokal na datu o pinuno.*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **ugnayan** ‚Äì *relationship, connection*  
- **mga tao** ‚Äì *the people*  
- **umiikot** ‚Äì *revolved around* (root: *ikot* = to turn, circle)  
- **barangay** ‚Äì *villages*  
- **pinamumunuan** ‚Äì *ruled by*  
- **lokal na datu o pinuno** ‚Äì *local chieftains or leaders*

**Grammar:**  
- *Ugnayan...umiikot* ‚Üí Subject-verb inversion: "The relationship revolved around..."  
- Passive construction: *pinamumunuan*.

**Translation:**  
> The relationships among the people revolved around the barangays led by local chieftains or leaders.

---

**5.**  
**Original:**  
> *Mayroon mang maliitang kalakalan sa pagitan ng mga karatig na nayon sa pamamagitan ng barter, walang malaking pamilihan o sentro sa lugar ng Bi√±an noong panahong iyon.*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **mayroon mang** ‚Äì *even though there was*  
- **maliitang kalakalan** ‚Äì *small-scale trade*  
- **sa pagitan ng** ‚Äì *between*  
- **karatig na nayon** ‚Äì *neighboring villages*  
- **sa pamamagitan ng** ‚Äì *through*  
- **barter** ‚Äì *exchange of goods*  
- **walang malaking pamilihan** ‚Äì *no large market*  
- **sentro** ‚Äì *center*  
- **noong panahong iyon** ‚Äì *during that time*

**Grammar:**  
- *Mayroon mang...walang...* ‚Üí concessive structure (although there was... there was no...)  
- *Pamilihan* = marketplace noun (root: *bili* = buy + suffix *-han*)

**Translation:**  
> Even though there was some small-scale trade between neighboring villages through barter, there was no major marketplace or center in the area of Bi√±an during that time.

---

**6.**  
**Original:**  
> *Sa kabila nito, mahalaga na ang Lawa ng Laguna sa pamumuhay ng mga sinaunang Tagalog dito, at itinuturing nilang tahanan ang mayamang kalikasan ng Bi√±an.*

**Vocabulary:**  
- **sa kabila nito** ‚Äì *despite this*  
- **mahalaga na** ‚Äì *already important*  
- **Lawa ng Laguna** ‚Äì *Laguna Lake*  
- **pamumuhay** ‚Äì *way of life*  
- **mga sinaunang Tagalog** ‚Äì *the ancient Tagalog people*  
- **itinuturing nilang** ‚Äì *they regarded as*  
- **tahanan** ‚Äì *home*  
- **mayamang kalikasan** ‚Äì *rich nature*

**Grammar:**  
- *Sa kabila nito...* shows concession (despite).  
- **itinuturing nilang tahanan** = *they considered [it] home* (verb + object focus).

**Translation:**  
> Despite this, Laguna Lake was already important to the way of life of the ancient Tagalog here, and they regarded Bi√±an's rich natural environment as their home.

### üî• Observations:
- **Many ongoing actions** (present aspect): nangingisda, nangunguha, nangangaso, umiikot, itinuturing ‚Üí shows daily life and continuous activities.

### üìà How the Verbs Are Built

---

#### üîµ 1. **maituturing** (could be considered)

```
Root: turing ‚Üí to consider
+ mai- ‚Üí maituturing ‚Üí can be considered (potential passive form)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **turing** = to regard, to consider
- **mai-** prefix = "capable of being..." (potential form)  
‚Üí maituturing = *can be considered*

---

#### üîµ 2. **nangingisda** (is fishing)

```
Root: isda ‚Üí fish
+ mang- ‚Üí mangisda ‚Üí to fish
+ nangingi- ‚Üí nangingisda ‚Üí is fishing (ongoing)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **isda** = fish
- **mang-** prefix = actor-focus for doing action related to object
- Repeated first syllable + "nang-" infix = ongoing action form

---

#### üîµ 3. **nangunguha** (is gathering)

```
Root: kuha ‚Üí to get
+ mang- ‚Üí mangkuha ‚Üí to gather
+ nangungu- ‚Üí nangunguha ‚Üí is gathering (ongoing)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **kuha** = to get
- **mang-** = actor-focus for action involving "kuha" (getting things)
- **nangungu-** = shows present/ongoing action repeated (habitual/continuous)

---

#### üîµ 4. **nangangaso** (is hunting)

```
Root: gaso ‚Üí to hunt (old word, now rare)
+ mang- ‚Üí manggaso ‚Üí to hunt
+ nangang- ‚Üí nangangaso ‚Üí is hunting (ongoing)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **gaso** = to hunt
- **mang-** = actor-focused prefix
- **nangang-** = continuous aspect, habitual hunting

---

#### üîµ 5. **umiikot** (is revolving)

```
Root: ikot ‚Üí turn, revolve
+ um- ‚Üí umiikot ‚Üí is revolving (ongoing)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **ikot** = to circle, revolve
- **um-** infix (actor-focus, natural action)
- Continuous "umi-" for ongoing movement.

---

#### üîµ 6. **pinamumunuan** (is being ruled)

```
Root: puno ‚Üí leader
+ (um) ‚Üí pumuno ‚Üí to lead
+ -an ‚Üí punuan ‚Üí place of leadership
+ pina- + (um) + -an ‚Üí pinamumunuan ‚Üí is being led
```

üß† Same structure as previous explanation.

---

#### üîµ 7. **itinuturing** (is being regarded as)

```
Root: turing ‚Üí consider
+ i- ‚Üí ituring ‚Üí to be regarded (command form passive)
+ itinu- ‚Üí itinuturing ‚Üí is regarded (ongoing passive)
```

üß† Meaning construction:
- **turing** = to consider
- **i-** = object-focus prefix
- **itinu-** = ongoing aspect passive

### üß† Bonus: Quick Cheat on "Mang" Verbs Ongoing Forms
Whenever you have **mang-** verbs in Tagalog, the pattern for ongoing tense is often:

> **mang- + first syllable duplication + "ng" insertion**

Examples:  
- *mangisda* ‚Üí **nangingisda**  
- *mangkuha* ‚Üí **nangunguha**  
- *manggaso* ‚Üí **nangangaso**  

The **-nang-** or **-nangu-** insertion shows that the action is currently happening!
